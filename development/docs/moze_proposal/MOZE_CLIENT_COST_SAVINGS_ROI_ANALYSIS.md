## 1.4 Efficiency Improvements (Non-Labour Value)

Beyond direct staff-hour savings, the ERP creates structural value that compounds over time:

**Centralized Data — No Duplicate Entry**
Currently, data exists in silos: each institute maintains its own registers, Excel files, and paper records. The same student's data is entered multiple times: at admission, in fee registers, in attendance registers, in exam registers, and in compliance reports. With the ERP, data is entered once at source and flows through every module automatically. At 25,000 students, this eliminates an estimated 1,25,000 individual re-entry events per year.

**Real-Time Trust Dashboard**
MOZE management today must send emails or visit campuses to understand financial position, attendance rates, or exam results across institutes. The management portal delivers a live Trust-level dashboard showing all 19 institutes in one view — financials, attendance heatmaps, fee collection status, and student strength — accessible from any device, at any time. The Trust Chairman can review cross-campus KPIs from a mobile phone during travel without waiting for a staff report.

**Automated Notifications**
The system sends SMS/email alerts automatically for:
- Fee due (7 days, 3 days, on due date)
- Attendance below 75% threshold (parent and student, via SMS and in-portal notification)
- Exam results declared (link to portal result page sent instantly)
- Admission status changes
- Leave approvals

At ₹0.18/SMS on DLT-registered templates, MOZE can send 55,00,000 automated notifications per year at ₹9,90,000 — replacing calls that would otherwise consume staff time.

**Student Self-Service Portal — The Single Biggest Footprint Reducer**
Students check attendance, download fee receipts, view results, download hall tickets, and submit grievances online — without visiting the college office. At 19 institutes with an average of 150 student queries per institute per week, this eliminates **~1,48,200 in-person admin interactions per year**, directly reducing the clerk headcount required at enquiry desks across all campuses.

**Timetable Automation**
Timetable generation across 19 institutes currently takes 2–3 weeks of coordinator time at the start of each semester. The ERP's conflict-resolution engine generates a clash-free timetable in minutes, which is immediately published to the student portal. Across 2 semesters × 19 institutes, this saves **~760 hrs/year**.

**Cross-Institute Analytics**
Questions that today require a Trust-office executive to spend 3–5 days compiling — "Which institute has the highest fee defaulter rate?", "Which department has the worst attendance?" — are answered in seconds from the management analytics dashboard.

# SECTION 2: THE PORTAL — WHY THE USER EXPERIENCE IS THE ROI MULTIPLIER

## 2.1 The Portal Is the Product

Most ERP implementations fail not because of missing features, but because of poor adoption. Staff and students use workarounds — WhatsApp groups, Excel sheets, paper slips — because the system is too difficult or too slow to use daily.

MOZE's ERP is designed from the ground up to avoid this failure mode. The interface that all 27,000 users — students, faculty, parents, and management — interact with is a **custom-built Vue 3 Single Page Application (SPA)** served from `/portal/`. Frappe's administrative backend (Frappe Desk) is visible only to the 5–10 IT administrators who manage the system. Every other user experiences a modern, purpose-built portal.

This is not a cosmetic difference. It fundamentally changes adoption rates, reduces training time, and drives the self-service behaviors that produce the cost savings quantified in Section 1.

## 2.2 Who Uses What

| User Group | Portal Experience | Daily Tasks |
|------------|------------------|-------------|
| **25,000 Students** | Student Portal — vibrant role-specific dashboard | Timetable, attendance, fees, exam results, hall tickets, library, hostel, transport, placement, grievances |
| **~1,500 Faculty** | Faculty Portal — teaching-focused workspace | Mark attendance, view timetable, upload marks, apply for leave, access workload summary |
| **~300 HODs / Coordinators** | HOD Portal — departmental management view | Department attendance, faculty workload, exam schedules, course outcomes |
| **~200 Admin Staff** | Admin Portal — operational workflows | Fee collection, admissions processing, exam coordination |

**Nobody in the day-to-day operations of MOZE's 19 institutes will ever need to learn or navigate Frappe Desk.** The portal abstracts away all technical complexity and presents only what is relevant to each role.

## 2.3 Portal Feature Summary and Value

### Single Login URL — Zero Confusion
All users access the portal at one address: `/portal/`. The system detects the user's role automatically and renders the appropriate dashboard. There is no separate "student ERP," "faculty ERP," or "parent portal" to remember. This alone eliminates a class of support queries that plague multi-URL deployments.

### Modern, Vibrant UI — Adoption by Design

| Design Element | Description | Business Value |
|---------------|-------------|----------------|
| **Vibrant, role-specific dashboards** | Custom color schemes and layouts per role — not default gray Frappe forms | Students and young faculty engage immediately; adoption requires no enforcement |
| **Mobile-responsive layout** | Fully functional on phones (375px+) and tablets | 25,000 students primarily use smartphones — portal works without a computer |
| **Dark mode support** | System-level and user-level dark mode toggle | Modern UX expectation for under-25 users; reduces eye strain for evening use |
| **Progressive Web App (PWA)** | Portal installs on phone home screen — no app store required | App-like experience without App Store submission delays or Google Play fees |
| **Real-time KPI counters** | Animated counters, live attendance percentages, fee collection progress | Management sees live numbers, not yesterday's report |
| **Drill-down charts** | Click any chart segment to view underlying data | Trust-level to institute-level to individual student in three clicks |

### Role-Specific Dashboards — Cognitive Load Reduction

A student does not need to see payroll settings. A parent does not need to see examination seating allocation. Each portal role exposes **only the data and actions relevant to that user**, reducing the learning curve to near zero. A first-year student can navigate their portal within five minutes without training — which is critical for adoption at scale with 25,000 users who change each year as new batches enroll.

### Accessibility Compliance (WCAG 2.1 AA)
The portal is built to Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.1 Level AA standards. This means:
- Screen reader compatibility for visually impaired students
- Keyboard navigation for users who cannot use a mouse
- Sufficient color contrast for readability in bright sunlight (relevant for students using phones outdoors)
- Text resize support without layout breakage

This is not a legal checkbox. MOZE's obligations under the Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act (RPWD) 2016 extend to digital services. An inaccessible portal creates legal exposure and excludes a portion of MOZE's student population.

## 4.2 Detailed Notes on Each Alternative

### Commercial SaaS — MasterSoft / CampusCare / Edumerge

These are the most common alternatives evaluated by Maharashtra institutions. Key points:

- **Pricing model:** Per-student per-year licensing, typically ₹500–₹3,000/student. At 25,000 students, MOZE would pay ₹1.25 Crore to ₹7.5 Crore per year in licensing alone.
- **Multi-institute:** Most charge per institute. At 19 institutes, setup fees alone can reach ₹50,000 × 19 = ₹9.5 Lakh, plus per-year fees.
- **Customization:** The vendor controls the feature roadmap. If MOZE needs a custom NAAC criterion or a Trust-specific report, it waits for the vendor or pays bespoke charges of ₹2–5 Lakh per feature.
- **Portal quality:** Many commercial ERP portals use traditional server-rendered HTML forms — the same basic interface architecture used in the early 2000s. Students and young faculty accustomed to Swiggy, IRCTC UX upgrades, and Zerodha dashboards find these interfaces difficult to engage with. Some vendors offer a "student app" as a paid add-on that must be maintained separately.
- **Frappe Desk exposure:** Vendors that use Frappe as their backend often give end users direct access to Frappe Desk — an administrative interface designed for developers and power users, not for students or parents. MOZE's users would face the same gray, form-heavy interface that IT administrators use. This is a known adoption failure point.
- **Data:** Data lives on vendor servers. On contract termination, MOZE receives a data dump — often in proprietary formats that are difficult to migrate.
- **Lock-in:** If MasterSoft raises prices by 30% in Year 3, MOZE must pay or re-implement from scratch at enormous cost.
- **References:** Multiple Maharashtra universities have migrated away from commercial SaaS after Year 2–3 price escalations of 40–80%.

### Custom Development from Scratch

- **Reality:** Building a multi-module ERP with a full custom portal for 19 institutes requires a dedicated team of 8–12 engineers for 18–36 months. At market rates in Pune, this means ₹25,000–₹40,000/engineer/month minimum.
- **Cost:** ₹10 engineers × ₹30,000/month × 24 months = ₹72,00,000 in salaries alone. Add infrastructure, testing, compliance research, project management, and frontend design: ₹1.5 Crore – ₹2.5 Crore minimum.
- **Portal cost alone:** A well-designed Vue 3 frontend with role-specific dashboards, mobile responsiveness, PWA, dark mode, and WCAG 2.1 AA compliance — built from scratch — typically requires 4–6 months of dedicated frontend engineering at ₹35,000–₹55,000/month per engineer. That is ₹8–16 Lakh for the portal alone, before a single backend feature is built.
- **Risk:** Custom builds are notorious for scope creep, timeline overruns, and key-person dependency. If the lead developer leaves, the project stalls.
- **No compliance pre-built:** SPPU affiliation formats, AICTE annual returns, NAAC SSR data structure — all must be built from scratch. Add ₹30–60 Lakh for compliance modules alone.
- **Verdict:** Higher cost, higher risk, longer timeline than the Frappe-based solution, with no proven reference implementations.

### Fragmented Tools (Separate Apps for Each Function)

- **Common setup:** ERP for HR, Tally for accounts, Excel for attendance, a separate app for fees, WhatsApp for notices.
- **Portal problem:** There is no unified portal. Students use one app for fees, another for results, a third for the library. Faculty use different tools for attendance, leave, and payroll. Parents have no digital access at all. Each interface is different; each requires separate login credentials. Adoption collapses under this complexity.
- **Data never integrates:** A student's attendance data cannot be cross-referenced with fee defaulter data. Faculty leave records are disconnected from timetable. NAAC requires consolidated data — which must be manually compiled.
- **Hidden costs:** Multiple subscriptions, multiple training programs, multiple vendor relationships, multiple points of failure.
- **Year 5 problem:** Each tool upgrades independently. Data formats diverge. Integration breaks. MOZE is locked into workarounds.
- **Verdict:** Appears cheap initially, becomes expensive and unmanageable at MOZE's scale of 19 institutes and 25,000 students.

### Status Quo (No ERP — Do Nothing)

This is not a free option. As quantified in Section 1, MOZE currently spends an estimated **₹2.16 Crore per year** in staff time, paper, error correction, and compliance effort. This cost:
- Grows at 8–10% annually as student numbers and regulatory requirements increase
- Creates compliance risk (NAAC visits, AICTE inspections) due to data gaps
- Limits MOZE management's visibility into Trust-wide performance
- Puts MOZE at a competitive disadvantage against digitized peer institutions in Pune
- Offers students no self-service capability — every query requires a physical office visit

**Doing nothing is the most expensive option — it just does not appear as a line item on any budget.**

## 5.2 Immediate Risks Without AMC (Month 1–6)

**Security Vulnerability Exposure**
Frappe releases security patches regularly. Without AMC, these are not applied. A single unpatched vulnerability in a system storing 25,000 students' personal data (Aadhaar numbers, financial records, health data) constitutes a DPDP (Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023) violation. Penalties under DPDP can reach ₹250 Crore. The risk is asymmetric: skip ₹7.35 Lakh AMC, face potential ₹250 Crore liability.

**Vue 3 / Vite Frontend Ecosystem Drift**
The portal frontend is built with Vue 3 and bundled with Vite. Both the Vue framework and the Vite build tool release regular updates. Browser vendors — Chrome, Safari, Firefox — also update frequently. Without active maintenance:
- A Chrome major version update may trigger CSS rendering differences or JavaScript API deprecations that break portal layout or functionality
- Safari on iOS (the primary browser for iPhone users, which cannot be changed) has historically introduced breaking changes to web APIs used in PWA and localStorage-dependent features
- Vue or Vite security advisories go unaddressed, creating supply-chain vulnerabilities in the portal's JavaScript bundle

The portal is what 27,000 users touch daily. A broken portal is a broken ERP from their perspective, even if the backend is functioning perfectly.

**Frappe / Python / MariaDB Incompatibility**
Server operating systems and database engines receive security updates that can break compatibility with older Frappe configurations. Without version management, MOZE's server could become non-functional after a routine OS security update.

**Compliance Format Changes**
NAAC revised its SSR framework in 2024 and continues to update criteria. AICTE updates its ERP data upload formats annually. SPPU changes its affiliation data requirements. These format changes require code updates — which are included in AMC and charged at ₹75,000–₹1,50,000 separately without it.

**Production Bug Discovery**
No software is bug-free at go-live. The first 6 months of production use across 19 institutes will surface edge cases specific to MOZE's workflows. Without AMC, each bug fix is a separate incident charge.

## 5.3 Medium-Term Risks Without AMC (6–18 Months)

**Portal Becomes Functionally Outdated**
As institute requirements evolve — new examination patterns, new hostel booking workflows, new placement reporting — the portal cannot be updated without fresh development engagement. Staff begin creating parallel processes (WhatsApp groups, Excel trackers) to compensate for the static portal. Within 12–18 months, the portal is no longer the system of record. This is how ERPs die: not from failure, but from abandonment.

**Accessibility Compliance Decay**
WCAG 2.1 AA compliance is not a one-time certification. As portal features are added or modified without accessibility review, the portal drifts out of compliance. Assistive technology (screen readers) update their behavior. Without AMC maintenance, MOZE's obligation under RPWD 2016 cannot be sustained.

**Performance Degradation**
At 25,000 students with 2+ years of data, the database grows to 15–25 GB. Without quarterly optimization (index rebuilding, query tuning, archiving), response times degrade. Pages that load in 0.5 seconds at go-live may take 8–12 seconds by Month 12. On mobile connections (which most students use), this is unusable. Students stop using the portal; foot traffic to admin offices resumes.

**Undetected Downtime**
Monitoring alerts are part of the AMC. Without monitoring, MOZE's system could be down for hours during a critical period — exam results day, fee deadline, NAAC inspection — before anyone knows. A 4-hour outage on fee deadline day affects 25,000 students and their families.

**Database Bloat**
Without routine archiving and cleanup, tables accumulate orphaned records, duplicate entries, and audit logs that multiply database size. This directly impacts system speed and backup times.

**Backup Integrity**
Automated backups run every night. But backups are useless if they are corrupt. Without quarterly backup-restoration testing, MOZE may only discover that 6 months of backups are corrupt when it needs them — after a server failure.

## 5.4 Long-Term Impact Without AMC (18+ Months)

**Portal Falls Behind Competitive Expectations**
Peer institutions in Pune (Symbiosis, MIT, MAEER's MIT, VIT Pune) are actively improving their digital student experiences. Students who experience a modern onboarding at admissions expect that quality to continue through their academic journey. A portal that looks and functions like it was built 3 years ago — with no updates, no new features, and degraded mobile performance — is a reputational issue in an era where students post reviews on Google and Shiksha.com before selecting a college.

**Upgrade Cost Compounds**
Every missed Frappe version update makes the eventual upgrade harder and more expensive. Every missed Vue 3 minor version makes the frontend migration to a future framework version (Vue 4 or equivalent) more costly. The rule of thumb: skipping 3 years of updates costs 3–5× the AMC to rectify (estimated ₹22–37 Lakh vs ₹22 Lakh in AMC paid).

**DPDP Regulatory Penalties**
The DPDP Act 2023 came into force in 2025. Educational institutions handling student data are Data Fiduciaries. Security breaches due to unpatched systems — in either the backend or the portal frontend's JavaScript dependencies — create direct legal liability. Even a single data breach notification can cost ₹5–10 Lakh in legal fees, notifications, and regulatory response — before any penalty.

**Data Quality Erosion**
Staff, encountering software limitations, create workarounds: offline Excel sheets, WhatsApp messages, paper records. Over 18 months, data quality in the ERP degrades. By Year 3, MOZE has the worst of both worlds: a running ERP and parallel manual systems — the very problem ERP was meant to solve.

## Savings Calculation Methodology

All savings calculations use a three-step approach:
1. **Baseline:** Estimate current time/cost using industry benchmarks and staffing ratios for Maharashtra educational institutions
2. **ERP State:** Estimate residual time/cost after ERP automation (typically 10–20% of baseline for automated tasks)
3. **Net Saving:** Difference, expressed in hours and rupees

Conservative figures are used throughout. For example, the attendance module savings calculation uses only clerk time (not faculty time) for the primary cost-avoidance figure, although faculty time is the larger component. The full figure is noted separately for completeness.

Portal-driven savings (admin foot traffic reduction, parent self-service) are calculated separately from core ERP module savings to allow transparent attribution.

## NPV Methodology

NPV is calculated using the standard discounted cash flow formula:
- All savings modeled as end-of-year cash inflows
- Discount rate: 10% (reflecting cost of capital for an educational trust borrowing from institutional lenders in Maharashtra in 2026)
- No terminal value assumed (conservative)
- Year 1 savings reduced to 60% of steady-state to reflect ramp-up period and portal adoption curve
